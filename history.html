My Journey to the world of Art and Painting

As a child, I used to pursue art as my hobby. I never had thought I would devote so much time to art as an adult and make it my full-time career. I used have a lot of interest in science as a child. I stood first rank in my class and I was the school-topper of in class X and XII. I have post-graduation in Science (Msc. Chemistry)

Painting was a hobby for me since childhood. There used to be a Bengali Art Teacher who used to come to my home once a week. I used to look forward eagerly to the weekly class of drawing and painting and I used to paint as a hobby during my leisure time after exams or during vacations.

In 1999, I got married to Dr. Jyoti Prakash. It was an arranged-cum-love marriage, so we didn't have much family support. My husband was a junior resident at RML Hospital Delhi and I was in my final year of P.G. I started to look for alternative sources of income. Then I started studying and experimneting art in various forms

I gathered information regarding art and painting from periodicals, newspapers and roadside book vendors. Gradually, it became a habit to collect prints of paintings done by various artists from old-magazines sold on the streets on Old Delhi on sundays.

It was a dream come true for me when I was able to display my paintings to the public for the first time in 2000 from my residence. The response to the exhibition was very good and it gave me a lot of confidence to paint professionally. In my first exhibition itself, I was able to get a few orders.

Now, after 22 years of teaching and learning I can safely say that in studying art, talent, imagination, intelligence, intense feeling, total sincerity and a truckload of patience are required along with with practice and hardwork.
